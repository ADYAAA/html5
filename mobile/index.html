Mobile ★ Boilerplate

A best practice baseline for your mobile web app.

Mobile Boilerplate is your trusted template made custom for creating rich and performant mobile web apps. You get cross-browser consistency among A-grade smartphones, and fallback support for legacy Blackberry, Symbian, and IE Mobile. You get an offline caching setup for free, fast button clicks, a media query polyfill, and many common mobile WebKit optimizations waiting for you. Use Mobile Boilerplate to start your mobile webapp quickly and immediately benefit from community best practices.

show v1.1 Thank you note

Since the launch of Mobile Boilerplate v1.0, we have received lots of attention, response and feedback. Compare to H5BP, MBP had a shorter iterative time, which is largely due to the fact that mobile development as a whole has a shorter history, a lot common practices are not separate from the best practices, and there is still room for better practices. In the past week or so, we have found that there has been many areas we could improve upon v1.0. Paul, Divya and I are aware of the expectations for MBP (due to the existing, much matured H5BP), we decide to have an immediate release of v1.1 to improve it's support and standard. With v1.1, we made significant enhancement to some key areas, better kick-ass stuff baked in.

We thank you
We must say that we are really grateful about all the help received from various individuals and experts, without their help and advice, MBP won't have such significant improvement in such short time.

Jeremy Keith
First, we want to thank Jeremy Keith, one of the most respected web developer in the industry. He raised the problem of whether MBP should include 'no-scale, min/max-scale' in meta viewport. Maybe because of my cultural background (just kidding), 'the greater good' is always the bigger concern', but I know to build a front-end default to promote an open/free web, just to focus on 'protecting the interest of the majority' won't be good enough, and excuses like because Facebook, Google and Yahoo! are doing so also won't be good enough for Jeremy, and it won't be good enough to us or to Boilerplate either. So I set to tackle this 'accessibilty for the minority' vs 'usability for the majority' issue. This problem really made me couldn't sleep well for a night, I came out with a solution on my blog to initiate a discussion and collaboration with Jeremy. After hours of trying, we made great progress, finally settled on a way to deal with it. You can read more here and here. Although there is still remaining issue, but I am really happy that we end the debate in a positive and constructive way.

Bryan Reiger
We want to thank Bryan Reiger on helping us to reaffirm our view to remove 'target-densitydpi=160dpi', Bryan went beyond answering our initial question and provided us great insights on his view about avoid dealing with pixel perfect CSS and many other issues to be aware of. A real thinker and a great expert in mobile, someone we should always learn from.

Joe Marini
Joe from Microsoft helped us a lot in knowing about 'device-min/max-width', and helped us come to the conclusion of dropping 'device-'. I am really happy to see Microsoft starts to engage with the community more. The IE Mobile Blog is a small step in blogsphere, but a huge step for Windows Mobile!!!

Scott Jehl
Scott from jQuery Mobile helped us a lot in dealing with cross-browser media queries polyfill, he made a lot awesome JavaScript helper classes to deal with Media Queries issues.

Andreas Bovens
Andreas Bovens from Opera Mobile also helped us a lot in knowing about viewport and media queries stuff, a true gentleman provided us a lot of great ideas.

Not just the experts, we also want to thank the individuals, daily kick-ass developers, heroes of the pre 1.0 era, Mathias Bynens and Alex Gibson helped a lot with touch icon boilerplate, Jonathan Neal helped a lot in the discussion about mobile viewport. Post launch, we have Jamund Ferguson and Frank Denis helped helped with many fixes, Sam Breed helped us with MBP helper class.

Paul, Divya and I have burned many weekends to bring boilerplate to you (H5BP and now MBP). I am really proud to be in such a great team, we will do the best we can to make mobile boilerplate better over the time.

Shi Chuan, Boilerplate Team

Why it is awesome

  • Cross-platform compatible (Android, iOS, Blackberry, Symbian)
  • CSS class to target IE Mobile 7
  • Home screen icon (Android, iOS, Symbian)
  • Cross browser viewport optimization for Opera Mobile, Android, iOS, IE, Nokia, Blackberry.
  • Optimized viewport scaling (Opera Mobile, Android, iOS, Mobile IE, Blackberry)
  • Option to enable iOS start-screen in full screen mode
  • Better font rendering on IE Mobile
  • Adaptable markup and CSS skeleton
  • CSS stylesheet for low-end devices
  • Mobile sitemap
  • Mobile MIME type support
  • Build tool for mobile
  • Fix iPhone reflow scale up bug
